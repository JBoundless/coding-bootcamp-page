HMTL Coding Bootcamp

The HMTL (Help Me to Learn) Coding Bootcamp is a new kind of bootcamp focused on Web Development and Software Development. Scroll on for more info.

Curriculum

The curriculum is divided into two parts: Web Development and Software Development. There are also three different paths: Frontend, Backend, and Full Stack, and students can switch anytime.

In addition to video lectures and hands-on learning, there are also collaborative projects that allow students to work with professional developers at major companies, including Google, Facebook, and Robinhood. Some of our adjunct faculty and mentors even work at those companies.

Admissions Process

Applicants must send over college transcripts and any other certificates they earned through online programs. They will then be directed to two rounds of interviews, and need to score at least eight points (out of ten) in each round to move on into the final. There, they will meet with the dean of admission for a formal interview. Decisions will be made one month before the start of formal instruction.

Tuition

The initial cost of the bootcamp is $3,500 (if accepted). Afterward, the full tuition can be paid off with rates that are determined based on the salary you earn. Our Finance group will help determine the timeframe for paying off your loans after graduation.

Locations

We have four locations, two in the U.S. and two in Canada. The U.S. locations are in San Francisco and New York City, while the ones in Canada are located in Vancouver and Toronto. We are working on adding several others in Los Angeles, Chicago, and Nova Scotia, and even London. In addition, we will soon be offering some of our courses in select university CS curriculums, including Stanford, MIT, and Boston University.
